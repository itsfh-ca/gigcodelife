Every gig platform has a version of the banner: decline a few offers and up pops a warning implying your account is on thin ice. It works because it's vague. Vague feels like a threat.

Read the actual terms and the picture changes. On most delivery apps, acceptance rate is decoupled from account standing entirely — it can gate access to a *priority* or *rewards* tier, but it does not get you deactivated. The things that actually deactivate accounts are documented and specific: completion rate below a hard floor after you've accepted, safety flags, fraud. Declining an offer you never accepted is none of those.

**The one exception worth respecting:** completion rate. Accept, then cancel, and that's a different number the platform takes seriously. Declining is free. Bailing is not.

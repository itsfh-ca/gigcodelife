I exported ninety days of trips and did the thing the apps never do for you: divided total pay by hours actually online, not just hours on a trip. The gap between those two numbers is the whole story.

Two findings held up across the quarter. First, my best-paid hours weren't the busiest ones — they were the *understaffed* ones, early mornings when fewer drivers were online and the same demand got split fewer ways. Second, "waiting online for an order" quietly ate about a fifth of my logged time and paid nothing, which drags the real hourly down far below the per-trip figure that looks so good in the app.

**The takeaway:** stop optimizing the trip. Optimize the hour. The single biggest lever wasn't driving faster or accepting more — it was only being logged on when the ratio of demand to drivers was in my favor.

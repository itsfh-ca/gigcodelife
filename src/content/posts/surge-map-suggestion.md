The heat map feels like a treasure map. It isn't. It's a rear-view mirror — it shows where demand *was* dense enough to raise prices a few minutes ago, and it's visible to every other driver at the same instant.

So the map creates the thing that kills it. Everyone sees the red zone, everyone drives toward it, and by the time the pack arrives the surge has been divided across too many cars or expired outright. You burned gas to reach a multiplier that's already gone.

**What actually works:** position for where demand is *about* to be — the venue letting out, the dinner rush a neighborhood over — not where the color already is. The best surge is the one nobody else has driven to yet.
